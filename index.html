They honeybee is the NJ state insect.

It makes honey for sweetening our meals and beeswax to smooth things out.

Honeybees also pollinate our flowers, including the NJ state flower, the violet.

Here are some interesting facts about honeybees:

  • Honeybees are incredibly important pollinators. They visit 50-100 flowers on a single foraging trip and can carry up to 35% of their body weight in pollen.
  • This pollination helps many plants, fruits, and vegetables reproduce and grow.
  • Honeybees have an advanced communication system. When a worker bee finds a good source of nectar, it performs a "waggle dance" to communicate the location, distance, and size of the food source to other bees in the hive.
  • This allows the hive to efficiently gather resources.
  • Honeybees never sleep - they take short breaks and periods of rest instead.
  • They have an internal GPS that allows them to find their way home from miles away, using the sun as a compass.
  • A single honeybee hive can contain 50,000-60,000 worker bees.
  • The queen bee is the only fertile female and her primary job is laying eggs. Worker bees can actually "fire" the queen if her egg-laying ability declines.
  • Honeybees produce honey in a variety of colors and flavors depending on the flowers they visit.
  • A single worker bee may fly up to 500 miles in its lifetime, visiting thousands of flowers.
  • Honeybees are the only insects that produce food for humans.
  • Honey is full of vitamins, minerals, antioxidants, and amino acids, and can be used as a natural energy boost and antimicrobial agent.

Overall, honeybees are fascinating, hardworking insects that play a crucial role in our ecosystem and food production. Protecting honeybees is important for the health of our planet.
